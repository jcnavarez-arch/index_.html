Notes
Fanmade derivative character inspired by early Miku fandom events.

Akita Neru (亞北ネル) — In October 2007, during the rapid rise of Hatsune Miku's popularity, a Japanese TV program portrayed Miku fans as unemployed “anime freaks.” Shortly after, Miku-related images temporarily disappeared from major search engines, and her Japanese Wikipedia page was removed. This triggered a major backlash (“flame-up”) on the 2ch forums, where rumors spread that a powerful advertising agency was conducting an anti-Miku campaign to suppress Vocaloid and indie music. Some users tried to calm the situation with posts like “Got bored, going to bed,” but they were mocked and labeled as “puppets of the agency.” Their behavior inspired a parody character named Akita Neru. Google, Yahoo! Japan, and Wikipedia later clarified that the issues were due to technical problems and copyright violations—not any campaign. However, the conspiracy rumor remains popular within the fandom. On April 1, 2008, Crypton officially adopted Neru as a derivative character of Miku.
