Hatsune Miku (初音ミク) — Hatsune Miku (CV01) is the first Japanese VOCALOID developed and distributed by Crypton Future Media, released in August 2007 for VOCALOID2 as part of the Character Vocal Series. Voiced by actress Saki Fujita, she became the seventh overall VOCALOID and the second released for the VOCALOID2 engine. Over time, Miku received many updates, including Append voicebanks, a VOCALOID3 English voice, and VOCALOID4 upgrades for both Japanese and English in 2016. She also received a Mandarin Chinese voicebank in 2017. In 2020, Miku's first non-VOCALOID voicebank, Hatsune Miku NT, was released for Crypton's Piapro Studio NT engine. Despite shifting toward their own software, Crypton continues collaborating with Yamaha and selling VOCALOID products. Miku NT was updated to Ver.2 along with Piapro Studio NT2 in March 2025. In August 2024, a refined edition of her V4X voicebank—Hatsune Miku SP—was released, improving tone and pronunciation. That same month, an updated VOCALOID6 version, Hatsune Miku V6 AI, was announced after strong creator demand. Originally slated for 2024, it was delayed to 2025 and then to early 2026, with an early-access demo planned for December 2025.
